# Understanding reports

A dated audit folder contains a summary, complete report, structured JSON, coverage inventory, manifest, specialist reports, findings, evidence, raw outputs, and remediation handoff.

## Severity

Critical, major, moderate, minor, and observation describe impact. Release-blocker status is separate and depends on the release context.

## Confidence

Confirmed, high, medium, low, and requires-manual-verification describe the strength of evidence.

## Positive findings

Sills records concrete patterns worth preserving. Positive findings use the same evidence and confidence rules as issues.

## Limitations

An inaccessible route, unavailable simulator, failed tool, missing role, or declined dependency is reported as a limitation, never converted into a passing result.

## Status conclusions and tasks

Every report now starts with a universal health level, a domain-specific conclusion label, status dimensions, and a prioritised Tasks section. Read the [status conclusions and tasks guide](/documentation/reports/status-and-tasks) for the complete contract.
