# Severity and release blockers

## Severity

- `critical`: immediate and severe harm, compromise, data loss, or complete failure of an essential capability.
- `major`: materially blocks or excludes users, breaks a primary workflow, or creates substantial maintainability or trust risk.
- `moderate`: meaningful friction, inconsistency, or risk with a workaround or limited scope.
- `minor`: localised quality defect with limited impact.
- `observation`: worthwhile context that is not a defect.

## Release blockers

`releaseBlocker` is independent from severity. Context may make a major issue a release blocker or a critical issue irrelevant to a particular release scope.

Consider task blockage, affected users, frequency, recoverability, legal exposure, data or financial risk, security impact, systemic reach, and available workarounds.
