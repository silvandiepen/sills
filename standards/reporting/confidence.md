# Confidence

- `confirmed`: directly reproduced or verified with reliable evidence.
- `high`: strong evidence with minimal uncertainty.
- `medium`: likely, but affected by incomplete runtime, source, or product context.
- `low`: weak signal retained for investigation, never presented as fact.
- `requires-manual-verification`: automation or source analysis cannot decide the question.

Confidence is never a substitute for severity and must not be inflated to make a report appear decisive.
