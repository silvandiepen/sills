# Finding confidence

Every finding states how strongly the evidence supports it:

- `certain`: directly reproduced or unambiguously proven by source or runtime evidence;
- `likely`: strong evidence with a remaining untested assumption;
- `possible`: a plausible risk requiring targeted verification;
- `insufficient-evidence`: the question was relevant but could not be tested responsibly.

Confidence is separate from severity. A possible critical risk is not silently promoted to a confirmed critical defect, and a certain low-severity issue remains certain.
