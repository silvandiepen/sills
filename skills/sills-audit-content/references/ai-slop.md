# AI-slop assessment

The phrase "AI-generated slop" may be used only for pervasive, evidenced incoherence. Do not infer authorship.

Assess four dimensions:

1. Product coherence: disconnected features, unclear purpose, contradictory workflows.
2. Visual coherence: generic repeated layouts, arbitrary decoration, inconsistent components, no relationship to audience or documentation.
3. Content coherence: filler, buzzwords, contradictions, unsupported claims, excessive headings, and copy detached from behaviour.
4. Implementation coherence: duplicated patterns, confused boundaries, generated repetition, unnecessary abstractions, and conflicting conventions.

A project-level conclusion normally requires strong evidence in at least three dimensions, repetition across the product, no intentional documentation explaining the pattern, and high or confirmed confidence.
