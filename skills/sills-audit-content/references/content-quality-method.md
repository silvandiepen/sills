# Content quality evaluation method

Use this reference for qualitative review. It supplements structural checks and does not replace audience or product context.

## Unit of analysis

Evaluate content in the smallest useful context:

- an individual label or message;
- a component and its surrounding state;
- a screen or document section;
- a complete task journey;
- repeated terminology across the product;
- a cross-surface promise or instruction.

Avoid judging isolated strings when surrounding content changes their meaning.

## Observation format

For each candidate issue, record:

1. exact wording or a short excerpt;
2. source or runtime location;
3. route, component, state, role, and locale;
4. intended user task;
5. audience evidence or assumption;
6. observed comprehension, action, trust, or recovery problem;
7. repetition and shared root;
8. recommendation and improved example;
9. verification method.

## Comprehension questions

- Can a first-time user explain what this means?
- Can the user distinguish the available choices?
- Are prerequisites and consequences stated before action?
- Are unfamiliar terms explained at the moment they matter?
- Does the wording name the object and action clearly?
- Does the user know what will happen next?

## Hierarchy questions

- Is the most important decision visible first?
- Are constraints placed before commitment?
- Can users scan headings and labels to understand the page?
- Is supporting detail progressively disclosed?
- Are duplicated or competing instructions increasing effort?

## Recovery questions

- Does the message explain what happened without blaming the user?
- Is there a concrete next action?
- Is retry behaviour and data preservation clear?
- Is help available when self-recovery is impossible?
- Does validation appear near the relevant field and persist long enough?

## Trust questions

- Are claims testable or appropriately qualified?
- Are costs, data use, permissions, limits, and irreversible consequences visible early enough?
- Does automated output explain uncertainty or provenance where needed?
- Does the product avoid false urgency, concealed defaults, and reassuring language unsupported by evidence?

## Rewrite guidance

An improved example should demonstrate the recommendation, not prescribe final brand copy without context. Preserve necessary legal, technical, and domain meaning. Prefer:

- concrete nouns and verbs;
- action labels that predict outcomes;
- one instruction per step;
- constraints near the relevant action;
- recovery language that explains the next move;
- plain language without removing essential precision.

## Readability tools

Readability, spelling, grammar, duplication, sentiment, and terminology tools may identify review candidates. They cannot determine audience fit, trust, hierarchy, tone, or task clarity alone. Record tool output as automated evidence and make the qualitative judgement separately.

## Manual review requirements

Require qualified manual review when:

- evaluating a language the auditor cannot judge confidently;
- legal, medical, financial, safety, or regulated meaning may change;
- cultural context materially affects interpretation;
- user research is needed to distinguish preference from comprehension failure;
- runtime state or role access is unavailable;
- the product's audience or purpose remains ambiguous.
